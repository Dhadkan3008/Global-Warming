GLOBAL WARMING

QUES - What is Global Warming??

ANS - Global Warming is the long-term heating of Earth's climate system observed since the pre- industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning,which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere.

# Man-made causes of Global Warming:-

1. Agriculture :- Various farming activities produces carbondioxide and methane gas. These lead to increase in the temperature of the Earth.

2. Overpopulation :- An increase in population means more people breathing. This leads to an increase in the level of carbondioxide,the primary gas causing Global Warming in the atmosphere.

3. Deforestation :- Plants take in CO2 and release O2 thereby maintaining environmental balance. This has led to an environmental imbalance, thereby giving rise to global warning.

4. Use of Vehicle :- Vehicles burn fossil fuels which emit a large amount of carbon dioxide and other toxins into the atmosphere resulting in a temperature increase.

5. Chlorofluorocarbon :- The CFCs have led to ozone layer depletion making way for the ultraviolet rays, thereby increasing the temperature of the earth.

6. Industrial Development :- With the advent of industrialization, The harmful emissions from the factories add to the increasing temperature of the earth.

# Natural causes of Global Warming:-

1. Volcanoes :- The ash and smoke emitted during volcanic eruptions goes out into the atmosphere and affects the climate.

2. Water Vapour :- Water vapour is a kind of greenhouse gas. Due to the increase in the earth’s temperature, more water gets evaporated from the water bodies and stays in the atmosphere adding to global warming.

3. Deforestation :- Plants take in CO2 and release O2 thereby maintaining environmental balance. This has led to an environmental imbalance, thereby giving rise to global warning.

4. Melting Permafrost :- Permafrost is frozen soil that has environmental gases trapped in it.It is present in glaciers. As the permafrost melts, it releases the gases back into the atmosphere, increasing Earth’s temperature.

5. Forest Blazes :- Forest blazes or forest fires emit a large amount of carbon-containing smoke.

QUES- What are the main efffeects of global warming??

ANS- Global Warming can result in many serious alterations to the environment, eventually impacting human health.It can also cause a rise in sea leve,leading to the loss of coastal land, a change in precipitation patterns, increased risks of droughts andfloods, and threats to biodiversity.
